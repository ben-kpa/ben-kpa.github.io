About

Hey there! My name is Ben and I like to design and build web applications that are related to academia. Right now I am still a medical student but every time I find some time (no pun intended) I teach myself web development.

I often wanted to create a better "study-learning-app", because I always felt that something was missing from applications that I was using to take notes or that I used for studying.

For example, Anki is "ok" for flashcards but I came across some deal-breaking issues. Reviewing flashcards needs a lot of time and you often get stuck in a review-loop if you don't configure it right. In general, it serves you the next card and you have no real control over this. Many other flashcard apps, again, don't give you much control over the next card you will see. So first of all, I wanted my app to support flashcards, but the user should have more control over them - similarly to how physical flashcards are. Then, any writing application is great for writing, but they are made just for that. Google Docs or Microsoft Word are excellent for taking notes, but I wanted an easy way to write notes, then create flashcards and keep them connected. Although there is more to the story than all of the above, these are some of the main reasons I created my Exambook Note-taking application.

It took me a lot of extra time to make it available for other people online, in a sustainable way. I am hoping that others will find value in what it has to offer. I also hope to profit from it, because that would allow me to improve it, and create other applications as well.

Ever since I started this design and development journey, I dream of having my own team of developers and engineers, who would  allow me to realize application ideas I've had over the years. Alone I can do only so much.
